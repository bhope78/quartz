#### What is the Business case for this project?  

The purpose of this site is to create a portfolio project to demonstrate technical skill while at the same building out the portfolio project in the context of a project management initiative.  

So the question that needs to be answered is do I frame the business case in the context of a portfolio project or do I frame in terms of an product or solution itself?

Since this a portfolio and I don't have additional users to work with.  Therfore, the product will be a single user application.  

In general since I am spending all this time building a solution and its a single user product it should be a solution that benefits me since I will be the primary user.

My thought for the solution that benefits me is a health tracker something where I can log various health metrics, exercise, nutrition.  While I get these through various means such as using myfitnesspal, my apple watch, the bluetooth weight scale they are somewhat centralized through apple health but other things are not such ph level monitoring, supplements.  Have a portal where I am logging all these items will give me a place where I can log and see results.

However since Power Platform is primarily a tool internal for companies and not and end product that complicates things a bit.  The typical organizational elements do not apply for this product since I am building basically a consumer product in an organizational structure.  

Do I follow a consumer product project path or do I follow an internal company project management path, Do I frame this project as a portfolio project or as internal solution?

#### My Decision Use a Hybrid Approach 

To understand the hybrid approach I will answer these questions:

***Why am I doing this?***

<u>Personally</u>
I want to take control of my health by tracking key metrics like diet, exercise, sleep, supplements, and symptoms in one place. Creating this system helps me maintain consistency, build healthier habits, and visualize long-term progress. 

<u>Professionally</u>
This project is a practical demonstration of my ability to plan, develop, and document a full-featured Power Platform solution. It also showcases my understanding of project management principles by organizing the build into phased development, using industry-aligned tools and methodologies (e.g. ClickUp, Scrum, Gantt, ALM).

***What value does it provide?***

<u>Personally</u>
The health tracker consolidates data from fragmented sources (like MyFitnessPal, Apple Health, and manual pH tracking) into one platform that is customizable, extensible, and tailored to how I personally measure wellness.

<u>Professionally</u>
The project functions as a live portfolio piece. It demonstrates technical proficiency across Power Apps, Dataverse, Power Automate, and Power BI — along with strong documentation, UX design, and project planning. It signals to potential employers that I can both build solutions and manage delivery.

***What problem are we solving?***

<u>Personally</u>
There is no single app that lets me track _everything_ I care about (like pH levels, supplement intake, symptoms, etc) in one view. Existing apps are siloed or inflexible. This solves the problem by giving me ownership of my own health data ecosystem.

<u>Professionally</u>
Professionally, this solves the problem of showcasing competency in a way that’s tangible, relevant, and aligned with how organizations use the Power Platform internally — while also highlighting creativity and initiative through a unique use case.
